General Impact of [CSLHY] project on Hung Dao Primary School Students & VinUni Students


Teachers and students had to transition to an online teaching and learning method two years prior as a result of the Covid-19 pandemic. English was then regarded as optional in order to ensure that students were taught essential subjects like Math and Literature in a condensed amount of time. As a consequence, some of the pupils probably lacked the foundation necessary to acquire English and even felt a little self-conscious when speaking it.

After the Winter English Project, students’ enthusiasm and proficiency in using English both greatly improved. When asked about their experience studying English, the majority of students expressed happiness and excitement. They boldly and frequently addressed us when they said “Hello” and “Goodbye” and other words like “describing objects” and “daily activities,” indicating that they felt more at ease using English in natural conversation. Unlike the first lesson, when the instructor announced, “Now, we’re going to learn English,” everyone in the class arrived prepared to learn with their own notebooks and pens. It was comprehensible because studying foreign languages in many Vietnamese institutions consists primarily of writing and grammar drills without a lot of speaking practice. We can therefore consider our modest achievement the shift in the students’ attitudes toward learning English there, from one that is quite passive to one that emphasizes more useful conversation.


For VinUni students who participated in this project, there were many unforgettable experiences for them. Within eight weeks, up to 50 students volunteered to teach 250 Hung Dao primary school children English, receiving more than 400 hours of community service learning credit. In addition to fulfilling the prerequisite for their CSL course, they are welcomed, shown kindness, and given sweet presents by the other students. When batch 1 was finished, some instructors and teaching assistants finished their duties and said goodbye to their beloved class, some students even hugged them and cried a lot. Some students even gave them hugs and shed many tears. Following several weeks of instruction, 500 Hung Dao students participated in an English Fair at the school with the support of nearly 70 instructors and Vinuni students. This was an opportunity for instructors and teaching assistants to observe their effort in teaching tutees to communicate English in daily life through funny games.
