Suggestions for future CSL projects


This Community Service Learning project at Hung Dao Primary School, Hung Yen has inspired us to think about a two-dimensional recommendation for future CSL projects.

Make it far: bring communicational English to more Vietnamese children in remote areas.


Having been born and raised in small provinces, we understand that children in remote areas generally have fewer educational opportunities than those living in the cities, especially in learning English. Besides, the lack of practice and the teaching program over-emphasizing on theory are the barriers making it even more difficult for children in rural areas to learn and actually use English for communication.


Meanwhile, children should be learning in fun, flexible ways to easily acquire knowledge, and use English in real life instead of just centering on examinations. This problem requires more English playgrounds with different approaches and creative teaching methods for children to practice and boost their confidence in using foreign languages.

Tien Lu district is just one of many rural areas in Vietnam facing that problem. From the success of CSH Hung Yen, we expect to inspire more VinUni students to continuously improve the project, and, with your youthful energy and spirit, bring more chances to learn English to children in many other areas. We, the CSL Hung Yen organizing team, are always ready if future leaders need any help with preparing and operating the projects.

Make it broad: Not only English.


English is of course essential in this globalized world. However, there are more things, that if we prepare children at an early age, could enlighten their curiosity and potential to grow and create values.

First of all, basic and interesting knowledge about a certain industry. It is not about the stressful theory or tons of principles, but about how amazing new technologies, innovations or practices are and how they can be used to create values and help people. This can be delivered by teaching and encouraging children about STEAM, basic programming from scratch, how art can make life more beautiful, etc. We have strong beliefs and expectations for the future creative projects of enthusiastic VinUni students.


Besides knowledge and experiments, skills play an important part in children’s study and development, especially in a fast-paced world that requires prompt adaptation and flexibility. The top skills that we can prepare the children with are recommended by many experts, such as critical thinking, leadership, emotional intelligence, etc. Equipping children with life skills is the best way to protect them, help them confidently enter life, form good habits as well as be independent and creative.
